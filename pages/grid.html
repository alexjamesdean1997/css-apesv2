GRID

The five basic families of apes, regular homogeneous layout "grid"

GORILLA

Gorillas are ground-dwelling, predominantly herbivorous apes that inhabit the forests of central Sub-Saharan Africa.

SNUB NOSED MONKEY

Snub-nosed monkeys are a group of Old World monkeys and make up the entirety of the genus Rhinopithecus.

CHIMPANZEE

The chimpanzee (Pan troglodytes), also known as the common chimpanzee, robust chimpanzee, or simply "chimp," is a species of great ape.

BABOON

Baboons are Old World monkeys belonging to the genus Papio, part of the subfamily Cercopithecinae which are found natively in very specific areas of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

CAPUCHIN

The capuchin monkeys are New World monkeys of the subfamily Cebinae.
